Gay marriage is not real

Marriage is about love and commitment—procreation isn't required. Infertile straight couples marry all the time without issue.
The state's interest in marriage stems from the unique ability of opposite-sex couples to naturally create children. Government incentivizes stable unions to handle the potential for unplanned kids. Same-sex couples don't create that biological possibility, so the procreative rationale doesn't apply.
But adoption and surrogacy exist—biology isn't the only way to have kids.
Adoption is valuable, but marriage law was never built around it. The primary link is tying sexual unions to potential parenthood to encourage responsibility. Redefining marriage breaks that public connection.
Modern studies show same-sex parents raise well-adjusted, happy kids—outcomes are equal or better in many cases.
Some research highlights gender complementarity: fathers often promote independence and risk-taking, mothers emphasize nurturing and emotional attunement. Missing one gender can lead to gaps (e.g., boys without fathers showing higher behavioral issues on average).
Those older studies are flawed or biased—current evidence supports same-sex parenting just fine.
The body of long-term data is still limited compared to opposite-sex families. When the state privileges marriage, it should prioritize structures with the most established evidence of positive child outcomes.
Marriage has evolved before (interracial, no-fault divorce)—expanding it to same-sex couples is just the next step toward equality.
Previous changes preserved the core: a male-female union linked to biological family formation. Extending to same-sex removes that foundational tie, making marriage arbitrary and weakening norms like monogamy and permanence that support child-rearing stability.
If marriage is just committed adult love, why limit it at all?
Because without a specific public purpose (like procreation/family incentives), marriage loses its societal force. The institution exists to channel behavior in ways that benefit children and stability—not just to celebrate any adult pair.
